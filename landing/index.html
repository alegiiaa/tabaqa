FAQ
Questions, answered.
Is Tabaqa a credit bureau?

No. Tabaqa is a cash-flow intelligence layer that complements the bureau. SIMAH tells you who has a record; Tabaqa scores the thin-file and no-record borrowers it can't rate — using consented, real-time bank and wallet behavior.

How is this different from Lean or Tarabut?

They're the pipe — the regulated rails that read the account. Tabaqa is the intelligence on top: cleaning, reconciliation, verification, and a scored decision. We're a channel for them, not a rival; the score can ride their connection.

Does Tabaqa ever move money?

Never. It's read-only AIS — no PIS, no payments, no account custody. It reads only what the user consents to, proves what it can against Masdr, and honestly flags what it can only infer.

What makes the income "verified"?

Every figure carries one of three provenance tags — amount-verified (matched to a Masdr payslip), source-verified (a known payer like Jahez), or inferred (a recurring pattern). The credit officer and the regulator see exactly how each number was earned.

Is it aligned with SAMA?

It's built consent-bound, read-only, and explainable — and the Tabaqa Risk flag is designed against SAMA's Counter-Fraud Fundamental Requirements (effective 13 Apr 2026). Tabaqa is built to sit inside a licensee, not to operate around one.
