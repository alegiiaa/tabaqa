## 3 · Bank+wallet legality (KSA) — the crown jewel, SAMA rulebook verbatim

### The statute chain ✅ 3-0 (three merged claims)
**Implementing Regulations of the Payments and Payment Services Law** (issued 13/6/2023, in force):
- **Art. 6 item 10** — "Payment Account Information Services" (AIS) = a **standalone licensable activity** (alongside payment initiation and e-money issuance).
- **"Payment Account" is provider-agnostic** (any account used for executing payment transactions); "Payment Account Service Provider" expressly ***includes but is not limited to*** licensed banks → **EMI wallets (urpay, Barq) and digital banks (stc bank, D360) are in AIS-readable scope by definitional chain** (mirrors the settled PSD2 reading).
- **Art. 96(1)** — account providers **MUST grant licensed AIS providers access on customer consent**, on an *"objective, non-discriminatory and proportionate basis."*
- **PAIS licence: SAR 20,000 issuance fee (Art. 23), NO joint-stock-company requirement (Art. 14)** — unlike major PI / major EMI / micro EMI.

- https://rulebook.sama.gov.sa/en/implementing-regulations-payments-and-payment-services-law (primary — all articles verbatim)
- https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2026/03/sama-new-licensing-framework-for-open-banking (secondary corroboration; also Pinsent Masons)

### Pathway proven end-to-end ✅ 3-0
SAMA regulatory sandbox (Lean's AIS products served insurance/lending/marketplace clients, **~1M bank accounts verified** — self-reported) → **SAMA's first two AIS licences announced Mar 26, 2026: Neotek** ("New Technology for Software Solutions") **+ Lean Technologies Saudi Arabia**.

- https://www.spa.gov.sa/en/N2546774 (primary — state press agency)
- Asharq Al-Awsat english.aawsat.com/business/5255553 · Sharikat Mubasher · Lean press release (above)

### Consent layer: SAMA policy + PDPL ✅ 3-0 / 2-1
SAMA's Open Banking Policy states verbatim: analysis of customers' financial-transaction data *"with customer consent — and offer tailored products,"* open banking *"will expand access to credit,"* consent standard = *"explicit and informed consent."* Aligned with **PDPL Art. 24** (explicit consent for credit data; effective 14 Sep 2023, grace ended 14 Sep 2024).
**REQUIRED SOFTENING:** this is aspirational policy language — say **"explicit SAMA policy endorsement," never "regulatory blessing."**

- https://sama.gov.sa/en-US/Documents/Open_Banking_Policy-EN.pdf (primary — quotes verified in the 6-page PDF)
- ICLG Saudi data-protection chapter 2025-26 · Morgan Lewis / Clyde & Co PDPL analyses

### Tarabut — the sandbox-to-certification datapoint ✅ 3-0 / 2-1
Sandbox test permit Nov 2022 (single-sourced to Tarabut's PR, 2-1) → **KSA Open Banking certification announced May 30, 2023** (multi-source, 3-0), launching AIS *within the sandbox*.
**Precision traps:** it was a framework-compliance certification + sandbox operation, **NOT a full licence** (those came Mar 2026). "Among the first" only as *first open-banking fintechs* (the sandbox ran since 2018, 45 permits by Q1 2023).

- https://www.tarabut.com/blogs/post/tarabut-open-bankin-certified-sama (primary) · Arab News arabnews.com/node/2312911 · IBS Intelligence · Gulf Business
- SAMA Annual Fintech Report 2022: https://sama.gov.sa/en-US/Documents/AFR22_en.pdf (confirms the permit mechanism, names no fintechs)

### 🎤 The judge-proof spoken answer (every beat maps to a 3-0 claim)
> "Three consented channels connect banks and wallets. **First**, a SAMA-licensed AIS aggregator — the exact activity SAMA licensed for the first time on March 26, 2026 (Neotek and Lean, after Lean verified ~1M accounts in the sandbox, including for lenders). **Second**, customer-permissioned statement upload, which our adapters parse today — legal under PDPL because the customer hands us their own data with explicit consent. **Third** is screen-scraping, which we refuse and the licensing regime is killing. Is it legal for wallets too? **Yes, by statute:** KSA's Payments Law defines a Payment Account provider-agnostically — any account executing payment transactions, expressly *including but not limited to* banks — so urpay and Barq (EMIs) and stc bank and D360 (digital banks) are in AIS scope, and Article 96 obliges providers to grant licensed AIS access on customer consent. **Our own path:** the dedicated AIS licence costs SAR 20,000 with no joint-stock requirement — or we ride a licensed aggregator until then."

**Volunteer if pressed:** mandatory OB Framework APIs reached banks first — live wallet-API pull is a *rollout* question, which is exactly why the statement-upload channel (our shipped adapters) matters today.
